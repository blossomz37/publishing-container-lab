THE EARTH WITCH’S TRUTH

Sacred Earth Botanicals was a haven of life even in the heart of winter. The garden thrived with a vibrancy that made Angelica’s heart ache with longing. Terra cotta pots brimming with desert flora lined the pathways—ocotillo stretching skyward, marigolds defying the season with their vibrant blooms, and cacti adorned with unexpected flowers.

Angelica hesitated at the gate, her gaze trailing to Moira, whose presence beside her felt reassuring. Together, they stepped through the iron entrance, the scent of creosote and sage embracing them like a warm, familiar blanket. Beneath these scents lingered something else—an electric tang, almost like the air before a storm.

Brenda Knight was tending to a young ocotillo, her hands moving with practiced grace that seemed to guide the plant’s growth. Her silver-streaked braid swung gently as she moved, and the plants around her seemed to pulse with the same energy.

"You’re brave, coming here after what happened last night," Brenda said without looking up. Her voice held a note of acknowledgment rather than reproach.

Angelica squared her shoulders, her resolve bolstered by Moira’s steady presence at her side. "I need to understand what happened. Why my magic didn’t work."

Brenda finally looked up, her eyes meeting Angelica’s with a depth that seemed to see right through her. "The land isn’t something to be controlled, Angelica. It’s something to be in harmony with."

Angelica glanced at a marigold, its petals shimmering with life that had eluded her own craft. "Your plants are thriving. How?"

"The land’s magic doesn’t fail—it withdraws when it’s out of balance," Brenda said, touching the soil with reverence. "Magic is a partnership, not a dictatorship."

Moira stepped closer, her gaze shifting between Brenda and Angelica. "The trails have been different too. The deer, the birds... even the plants are behaving strangely."

Brenda nodded, acknowledging Moira’s observations with a respect that Angelica hadn’t noticed before. "The signs are everywhere if you know how to listen. The land speaks in many voices."

Angelica frowned, the weight of her grandmother's teachings settling heavily on her shoulders. "I thought I was listening. But... maybe I was too focused on the spells."

Brenda’s expression softened. "Your grandmother knew that spells are just one part of magic. She listened first, then acted. That’s why her magic was so powerful."

The morning sun caught in Moira's hair as she bent to examine a cluster of marigolds. "So, what do we do? How do we help restore balance?"

Brenda smiled, a gesture that seemed to light up her entire face. "We listen. We bring together what’s been divided—wild magic and crafted spells. And we remember that the land must be willing."

Angelica nodded slowly, understanding dawning like the morning light. "I want to help, but I don’t know how to bridge that gap."

"That’s why we work together," Brenda said, her voice imbued with an unshakeable confidence. "Each of us brings something unique to the table."

Moira straightened, her eyes meeting Angelica’s with a warmth that spread through Angelica like a healing balm. "We’ve got your back."

As they prepared to leave, Brenda handed Angelica a small, clay pot containing a seedling, its roots wrapped in dark, rich soil. "Feel this," she instructed. "Let it teach you. Magic isn’t just about power—it’s about connection."

Angelica cradled the pot, the soil cool against her palms. Something within the seedling pulsed gently, a rhythm that matched her heartbeat. It was an invitation, a promise of what could be if she was willing to listen.

"Thank you," Angelica said, her voice carrying a newfound resolve. "I’ll see you both at dawn."

Together, they left the garden, stepping back into the crisp winter air. Angelica felt a sense of purpose settle over her, the path ahead illuminated not just by the morning sun, but by the understanding and support of those walking beside her.

DAWN'S PROMISE

The walk back from Cathedral Rock was a quiet one, each step crunching softly on the frost-laden path. Angelica felt the weight of her failed spell in the air, like the hollow calm after a storm. Yet, Moira's steady presence beside her offered a comforting anchor, something real and grounding as her magical certainties unraveled.

They paused at the gates of Sacred Earth Botanicals, where warm light spilled from the windows, cutting through the early morning shadows. Inside, Brenda moved among her thriving plants like a shadow herself, her silver braid catching the glow of dawn.

"She's expecting us," Moira murmured, breaking the silence.

Angelica touched her grandmother's bracelet, feeling its familiar warmth, and wondered aloud, "How did she know?"

"The land tells her things," Moira replied, a hint of admiration in her voice. "Just like it's been trying to tell us."

As the gate swung open without a sound, they entered the garden where desert marigolds bloomed defiantly against the winter chill. Each flower seemed to thrum with its own inner light, a magic so different from Angelica's practiced spells it made her chest ache with a newfound understanding.

Brenda was waiting for them, her hands gently tending to a marigold that glowed with impossible vitality. "The land speaks in many voices," she said, looking up to meet their gaze. "Sometimes through the plants, sometimes through the animals. And sometimes—" she glanced at Moira "—through those who know how to listen."

Angelica clutched her craft bag closer, feeling the weight of her remaining tools. "I thought I was listening. All those years studying my grandmother's grimoire, perfecting her techniques..."

"Your grandmother understood something you're just beginning to learn," Brenda said, her voice gentler than Angelica had ever heard it. "Magic isn't just about knowledge passed down. It's about wisdom earned."

The first rays of sunlight caught the frost on the garden's gate, turning it to diamonds. Moira stepped forward, her ranger's instincts alert. "The animals are moving differently now. Even the morning birds have changed their songs."

"Because they feel it too," Brenda nodded. "The imbalance. But—" she touched another thriving plant, its leaves practically humming with life, "there's still hope. If we work together."

Angelica felt something shift inside her, like a key turning in a lock. She looked at Moira's steady presence, at Brenda's wild-growing magic, and at her own depleted but determined craft. "What do we need to do?"

"We need to return to Cathedral Rock," Brenda said, already gathering supplies from her workbench—dried herbs that glowed with their own inner light, crystals etched with ancient symbols, and a small clay pot holding fresh soil. "At dawn. When the veil between crafted magic and wild energy is thinnest."

Moira moved closer to examine the herbs. "Like how the deer know when to change their grazing patterns," she offered. "Or how the desert marigolds bloom even in winter, if the conditions are right."

"Exactly." Brenda's eyes held approval. "Nature doesn't force. It adapts. Flows. Listens."

Angelica touched her grandmother's bracelet again, feeling its familiar warmth. But now she noticed something else—how the silver seemed to pulse in rhythm with the thriving plants around them, how its magic felt more like a conversation than a command.

"Dawn at Cathedral Rock," she said quietly. "When the veil is thinnest between crafted magic and wild energy."

Brenda nodded, wrapping her supplies in a cloth that shimmered with protective spells. "Bring what's left of your grandmother's tools. Not to command—to listen."

"I'll meet you both there," Moira said, adjusting her ranger hat. "I know where the deer have been avoiding. It might help us understand the disrupted patterns."

As they prepared to leave, Angelica caught sight of herself in one of Brenda's mirrors—and for the first time, she saw not just a witch trying to preserve her grandmother's legacy, but someone ready to help it grow into something new.
